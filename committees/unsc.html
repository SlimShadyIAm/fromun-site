United Nations Security Council


The United Nations Security Council is an organ of the United Nations created to certify World Peace and approve changes (such as addition of members) to the UN Charter. The council conventionally comprises of 15 members out of which 5 are permanent and the dominant democracies of the world and 10 are non permanent members, regionally elected to serve a 2 year term. The UN Security Council inspects the peacekeeping operations and is the only body of the UN which has the authority to issue binding resolutions to all its member states. Thus confident and dominating debaters have a perfect committee to join to ensure Global Peace and Tranquillity.
